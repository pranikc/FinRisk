Material M&A activity affecting the $10B–$50B US bank target list (as of 2026-04-23):

- **Pinnacle Financial Partners + Synovus**: announced merger, expected close Q1 2026. Combined entity becomes a top-tier Category IV candidate. Shellie Creson named CRO of combined company (formerly Synovus CRO). Implication: treat as ONE target, not two. Post-merger integration = massive risk-system rationalization opportunity (but they're busy and hard to sell into for 12 months).
- **South Plains Financial + BOH Holdings**: announced Dec 2025. Small deal but South Plains + Houston market entry.
- **Prosperity Bancshares + Southwest Bancshares**: completed 2025.
- **Ongoing post-SVB consolidation theme**: banks crossing $10B (CFPB oversight trigger) and $50B / $100B (Category IV enhanced prudential standards) thresholds via M&A is a 2026 tailwind for risk-tech buyers.

**Why this matters:** M&A integration creates (a) temporary chaos that blocks new-vendor evaluation for ~12 months, but (b) creates a forced repricing of risk infrastructure 18–24 months later. An acquirer that just crossed a regulatory threshold is a high-intent buyer.

**How to apply:**
- De-prioritize banks mid-integration (first 12 months post-close) — they won't buy.
- Actively target banks that crossed $10B or $50B in the last 24 months — they have new reg obligations and are actively shopping.
- Watch FDIC merger approvals / Fed applications for upcoming threshold-crossers — free leading indicator.
